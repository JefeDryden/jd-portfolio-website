50,000kg Metallic Phthalocyanine Dye Plant Design

Capstone chemical engineering plant design for industrial dye production

Capstone Project Chemical Engineering, Process Design, Industrial Scale View Full Report

Project Overview

This capstone project involved the complete design of an industrial-scale chemical plant capable of producing 50,000 kg per year of metallic phthalocyanine dye. The project encompassed all aspects of chemical plant design, from process development to economic analysis, demonstrating comprehensive chemical engineering expertise.

Metallic phthalocyanines are important industrial dyes used in printing inks, plastics, and coatings. The design focused on creating an efficient, safe, and economically viable production facility while meeting environmental regulations.

Plant Design Components

Process Development

Developed optimized reaction pathways and conditions for phthalocyanine synthesis with maximum yield and purity.

Equipment Design

Sized and specified all major equipment including reactors, heat exchangers, distillation columns, and crystallizers.

Process Control

Designed control systems and instrumentation for safe and efficient plant operation.

Environmental Compliance

Integrated waste treatment and emission control systems to meet environmental regulations.

Technical Specifications

  • Production Capacity: 50,000 kg/year of metallic phthalocyanine dye
  • Product Purity: >99% purity specification for industrial applications
  • Plant Operation: Continuous process with batch crystallization
  • Raw Materials: Phthalic anhydride, urea, metal salts, and catalysts
  • Utilities: Steam, cooling water, electricity, and nitrogen systems
  • Safety Systems: Emergency shutdown, pressure relief, and fire suppression

Engineering Analysis

Mass & Energy Balances

Performed detailed material and energy balance calculations for all unit operations.

Heat Integration

Optimized heat exchanger network to minimize utility consumption and operating costs.

Economic Analysis

Conducted comprehensive economic evaluation including CAPEX, OPEX, NPV, and payback period.

HAZOP Study

Performed hazard and operability analysis to identify and mitigate process risks.

Project Achievements

  • Designed a plant with 15% lower capital cost than industry benchmarks
  • Achieved 92% overall process yield through optimization
  • Reduced energy consumption by 20% through heat integration
  • Implemented zero liquid discharge system for environmental compliance
  • Developed comprehensive P&ID drawings and equipment specifications
