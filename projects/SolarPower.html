Solar Flyback Micro-Inverter Design

Electrical engineering design for renewable energy micro-inverter system

School Project Electrical Design, Solar Power, Renewable Energy

Project Overview

This project involved the design and development of a flyback micro-inverter for solar power applications. The goal was to create an efficient, reliable system that could convert DC power from solar panels to AC power for grid connection or local use.

The design focused on optimizing efficiency, minimizing harmonic distortion, and ensuring reliable operation under varying solar conditions, contributing to renewable energy solutions.

Technical Design Components

Power Electronics

Designed flyback converter topology with optimized switching frequency and component selection for maximum efficiency.

Control System

Implemented MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking) algorithm to optimize solar panel output under varying conditions.

Protection Circuits

Integrated overvoltage, overcurrent, and thermal protection to ensure safe and reliable operation.

Grid Synchronization

Developed phase-locked loop (PLL) for grid synchronization and power quality compliance.

Design Specifications

  • Input voltage range: 20-45V DC (typical solar panel output)
  • Output: 120V AC, 60Hz grid-compatible
  • Peak efficiency: >95%
  • Total harmonic distortion: <5%
  • Integrated MPPT for optimal energy harvesting
  • Compact design suitable for panel-level integration

Design Process

The project followed a systematic engineering design process:

  • Requirements Analysis: Defined specifications based on solar panel characteristics and grid requirements
  • Circuit Design: Selected flyback topology for isolation and voltage conversion flexibility
  • Component Selection: Chose components balancing performance, cost, and reliability
  • Simulation: Used SPICE simulation to verify design performance
  • PCB Layout: Optimized layout for EMI reduction and thermal management

Project Impact

This project demonstrates the application of power electronics in renewable energy systems. The micro-inverter design enables distributed solar power generation with improved efficiency and reliability compared to traditional string inverters, contributing to more accessible and efficient solar energy adoption.
